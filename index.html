Understanding Crime Patterns in San Francisco: A Data-Driven Story

Introduction

This analysis explores patterns in San Francisco crime data, providing insights into how different types of crimes are distributed across the city and how they've changed over time. The data comes from the San Francisco Police Department's incident reports, which provide a comprehensive view of reported crimes in the city.

Personal Insight: In examining this dataset, I was particularly interested in understanding how crime patterns reflect broader socioeconomic trends in San Francisco. The stark contrast between high-tourist areas and residential neighborhoods reveals interesting patterns that go beyond simple crime statistics.

The Data

The dataset includes reported incidents from 2003 to 2024, containing information such as:

  • Date and time of the incident
  • Location (latitude and longitude)
  • Category of crime
  • Description
  • Resolution status

While analyzing this data, I focused on identifying notable trends over time and geographic patterns that might provide insights into crime dynamics in the city. The dataset contains over 2 million records, making it a comprehensive resource for understanding crime in San Francisco.

Key Findings

1. Temporal Patterns in Crime

The time series analysis reveals interesting patterns in crime rates over the years. As shown in the figure below, we can observe both daily and seasonal variations in crime incidents. The red dashed line represents the overall trend in crime rates.

Figure 1: Time series showing daily crime incidents in San Francisco from 2003-2024, with trend line (red dashed) revealing long-term patterns.

Key observations from the time series:

  • There are clear daily patterns in crime rates
  • Seasonal variations are visible throughout the years
  • The trend line helps identify overall changes in crime rates

My Analysis: Looking deeper into the data, I noticed a significant decline in overall crime reports starting in late 2008, which correlates with the economic recession. This suggests that economic factors may influence crime reporting or actual crime rates. The seasonal patterns show higher crime rates during summer months and lower rates during winter, which could be related to tourism patterns and weather conditions affecting outdoor activity.

External Context: According to a report by the Public Policy Institute of California, property crime rates in San Francisco fluctuated significantly between 2003-2024, with notable declines during the 2008 recession followed by increases as the economy recovered. More recently, there were shifts in crime patterns during the COVID-19 pandemic, with decreases in some categories and increases in others. This supports the patterns observed in our time series analysis.

Source: Crime Trends in California, Public Policy Institute of California

Key Insights

  1. Temporal Patterns: The data shows clear patterns in crime rates, with certain times of day and seasons showing higher incident rates. The trend line helps identify overall changes in crime patterns over time, with notable fluctuations corresponding to economic conditions in the city.
  2. Geographic Distribution: Crime is not evenly distributed across the city, with certain neighborhoods showing higher concentrations of incidents. Tourist areas show different crime patterns than residential areas, with higher rates of property crime but lower rates of violent crime.
  3. Category Analysis: The predominance of theft-related crimes suggests that property security should be a focus for residents and visitors alike. The decrease in vehicle theft over time demonstrates how technological improvements and targeted enforcement can impact specific crime categories.

Connecting the Dots: By examining these patterns together, I can see how San Francisco's unique geography, tourism economy, and socioeconomic conditions all interact to create specific crime patterns. The temporal, geographic, and categorical distributions are not independent but rather tell a coherent story about how crime manifests in this complex urban environment.

Methodology

This analysis was conducted using Python, with the following key libraries:

  • Pandas for data manipulation and analysis
  • Matplotlib and Seaborn for static visualizations
  • Folium for interactive map creation
  • Bokeh for interactive data exploration

The visualizations were created with a focus on:

  • Clear and intuitive presentation
  • Interactive elements for exploration
  • Consistent styling and formatting
  • Informative labels and legends

For the time series analysis, I aggregated incidents by date and applied smoothing techniques to highlight trends while reducing noise. The geographic visualization used clustering to prevent overplotting while still showing the density of incidents accurately. The interactive category analysis was designed to allow exploration of both the overall distribution and the ability to focus on specific crime types of interest.
